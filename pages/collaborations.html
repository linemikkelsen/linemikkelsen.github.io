Collaborations
Much of my work is collaborative and organized around four collaborations:
Kalaallisut Syntax Semantics of Sameness and Difference Karuk Language Revitalization Chochenyo Language Revitalization

Kalaallisut syntax

Together with Kalaallisut speaker Ellen Thrane and fellow syntacticians Emily Clem, Amy Rose Deal, and Michelle Yuan, I work on topics in Kalaallisut syntax. My work with Amy Rose Deal centers on syntactic ergativity and my work with Emily Clem and Michelle Yuan on patterns of apparent cross-clausal agreement.

Semantics of sameness and difference

Together with Daniel Hardt I have investigated the semantics and pragmatics of expressions of sameness and difference.

Karuk language revitalization

I have a long-standing collaboration with Karuk language teacher Florrine Super and her sons Ivan Super and Issac Super. Florrine, Ivan and Issac are teaching and promoting Karuk language and culture in Kahtishraam (Yreka) California in schools and community classes. Our collaboration supports Karuk language learning and educational opportunities more broadly through curriculum development and youth college preparation activities.

I also work on language reclamation with Karuk cultural practitioners Dixie Rogers and Claudette Starrit of Sacramento. Dixie and Claudette regularly do basket weaving demonstrations at UC Berkeley and share their experiences working in language and culture with students.

Chochenyo language revitalization

UC Berkeley is situated on the land of the Chochenyo-speaking Ohlone people, who are the successors of the federally recognized Verona Band of Alameda County. With Ohlone activists Vincent Medina and Louis Trevino I am involved with activities that strengthen Chochenyo language, culture and sovereignty.
